I consider that Nebojsa Zivkovic website is a good exmaple of repetition, because at every section you can see a reference to his name. You have many options, but all of them have the reference to his last name.

For me Come unto Christ page, from the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints website, is a good example of Hick's Law and also of Fitt's Law, because it is very clear that it is a missionary site, and it offers three basic menu buttons, although that there are many functionalities at the navigation bar.For me it is a very clear reference that less is more and that if the buttons are more accesible it will be easier for the user to interact.

Dell Technologies site is a very good example of white space, they show you their bussiness, with big images, and some supporting material, but it is a very clear site. They have lots of space between the image and the information. It is a serious type of site, but if you scroll you can find some movement in the design.
